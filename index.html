Smoking-Related Illnesses

Visual insights into the health risks caused by smoking

Lung Cancer

Smoking causes nearly 90% of lung cancer cases. It’s the leading cause of cancer death worldwide.

Bladder Cancer

Harmful chemicals from tobacco are filtered by the kidneys and stored in the bladder, increasing cancer risk.

Throat and Mouth Cancer

Tobacco chemicals damage cells in the mouth, throat, and voice box, leading to painful and disfiguring cancers.

Cataracts and Vision Loss

Smoking contributes to cataracts and macular degeneration, leading to vision impairment and blindness.

Peripheral Artery Disease (PAD)

Smoking restricts blood flow to limbs, increasing the risk of infection, pain, and even amputation.
